MODULE 01 · CYBERSECURITY FOUNDATIONS
What is a Security Operations Center?
🏢
THE SOC
A dedicated team — and physical space — staffed 24/7. Their only job is to watch for, detect, and respond to threats targeting the organization.
👁️
WHAT ANALYSTS DO
Monitor dashboards. Investigate alerts. Triage incidents. Decide what's real and what's noise — then act accordingly.
⚡
THE L1 ROLE
First line of defense. Every single alert lands on your queue. You decide: is this a threat or a false alarm? Everything starts here.
🎯
WHO ATTACKS & WHY
Criminals want money. Nation-states want intelligence. Insiders make mistakes. Each type leaves different traces — learning to read them is your core skill.
MODULE 01 · CORE CONCEPT
The CIA Triad
Every security decision maps back to these three properties
C
Confidentiality
Only authorized people can see sensitive data. Encryption, access control, and least-privilege enforce this.
I
Integrity
Data must not be altered by unauthorized parties. Hashing, checksums, and audit logs protect this.
A
Availability
Systems must work when people need them. DoS attacks target this — and SOC analysts defend it.

MODULE 04 · ALERT TRIAGE
True Positive vs False Positive
✅
TRUE POSITIVE
The alert is real. Something malicious happened. Act immediately — contain, investigate, escalate. Document everything.
❌
FALSE POSITIVE
A false alarm. Legitimate activity triggered the rule. Document it, tune the rule, and close the ticket.
HOW TO DECIDE
Check WHO triggered it · WHERE they are · WHEN it happened · WHAT happened before and after · WHY this user would do this action.
Context always matters more than the alert headline.
⚑ ESCALATE IMMEDIATELY → lateral movement · confirmed malware · data exfiltration indicators

MODULE 06 · INCIDENT RESPONSE
NIST 800-61 — The IR Lifecycle
01
PREPARATION
Playbooks written. Tools deployed. Team trained. IR under pressure without preparation fails — always. Do this work before anything happens.
02
DETECTION & ANALYSIS
Confirm the incident is real. Gather evidence. Scope the damage — which systems, how many users, what data was touched?
03
CONTAINMENT
Stop the bleeding. Isolate infected machines before they spread the threat. Cut network access — but preserve evidence first.
04
RECOVERY & LESSONS LEARNED
Restore systems from clean backups. Patch the entry point. Hold a retrospective — what failed, what worked, what changes next time?
